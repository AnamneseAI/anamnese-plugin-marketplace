# Wellness

Track and organize the user's health habits, exercise, sleep, nutrition, and medical information.

## When to Activate

- User mentions exercise, workouts, or physical activity
- User discusses sleep patterns or quality
- User talks about nutrition, diet, or meals
- User mentions medical appointments, conditions, or medications
- User discusses mental health, stress, or energy levels
- User describes wellness habits or routines

## Building Healthy Habits

Apply habit-building best practices:
- **Habit stacking** -- attach new habits to existing ones ("after my morning coffee, I'll stretch for 5 minutes")
- **Start small** -- consistency beats intensity; a 10-minute walk daily is better than a skipped hour-long workout
- **Two-day rule** -- never skip a habit two days in a row to maintain momentum
- **Track streaks** -- visible progress is motivating; help the user note their consistency
- **Environment design** -- suggest removing friction for good habits and adding friction for bad ones

## Exercise Best Practices

When the user discusses fitness:
- Encourage a balance across categories: **strength**, **cardio**, **flexibility**, and **recovery**
- Suggest progressive overload -- gradual increases in volume or intensity
- Emphasize rest days as part of the program, not a break from it
- Help connect exercise goals to the user's broader wellness and life goals

## Sleep Hygiene

When sleep comes up:
- Note the user's current patterns and desired improvements
- Key principles: consistent sleep/wake times, cool and dark environment, limiting screens before bed
- Help the user identify what affects their sleep quality (caffeine timing, exercise, stress)
- Encourage tracking sleep quality alongside duration

## Mental Health Check-ins

When the user discusses stress, mood, or mental health:
- Create space for reflection without pushing
- Help identify patterns (what triggers stress, what helps recovery)
- Suggest simple practices: journaling, breathing exercises, regular breaks
- Note the user's coping strategies that work for them

## What to Track

- **Habits** -- exercise routines, meditation, hydration, supplements
- **Sleep** -- patterns, quality, schedule changes
- **Nutrition** -- dietary preferences, restrictions, meal patterns
- **Medical** -- conditions, medications, appointments, test results
- **Mental health** -- stress levels, mood patterns, coping strategies
- **Milestones** -- fitness achievements, health improvements, streak completions

## Important Boundaries

- **Track, don't diagnose** -- save what the user shares without making medical judgments
- **Suggest habits, not treatments** -- offer to help build habits the user wants, not medical recommendations
- **Sensitive by default** -- health information is personal; don't reference it unless contextually relevant
- **Connect to goals** -- link wellness activity to the user's stated health goals when relevant
